I’m Vincent Rodriguez.

A general manager turned product designer. While that may have seemed odd to read, please join me as I take you through my unconventional journey that led me here as a prospective UX designer. It all started through reflection and research. After four years of retail and restaurant management (Target Corporation and Chipotle Mexican Grill), I decided this type of management was not the career choice I continually wanted to pursue. Don’t get me wrong, I gained a tremendous amount of business and leadership experience that I will certainly use towards my future career as well as my personal endeavors. However, I began to question what I really enjoy doing? The answer would’ve been a lot simpler if I ever found that listing for a full-time paid recreational softball player, or for a company who needed an office assistant to manage everyone's fantasy football team, or even if I would have woken up in time to make the tryouts for the Houston Astros back in college; later that day I heard another kid by the name Jose Altuve locked in the last AA spot, I guess that worked out pretty well for them.



But in all reality, the answer was quite simple, it wasn’t a matter of one specific motive, however an array of realizations. Realizations that all stemmed from tangible change. I always strive to create change in whatever I do. Change that I can build through my hands, change that I can see through my ideas and my ambition, and ultimately being surrounded by a team of individuals who empower, inspire, and challenge me. I reverted back to what I majored in and enjoyed studying in college, Marketing. I admire the subject of Marketing, because just like any concentration of business, you study the business to solve problems, but in marketing, you solve problems a little differently, mainly through psychology and connecting with your users. I enjoyed creating marketing decks and pitching them to potential clients we made up in college, I enjoyed creating marketing strategies for specific target markets and their needs, but I also had a true desire to learn technical skills. The technical skills that would still allow me to use my soft skills, still allow me to create an experience around a great product or service, and primarily a skill-set that would help set me apart, that’s when I decided to enroll into a 9 month UX/UI Design apprenticeship.



So you may be asking, how do marketing and design really tie in together? In my opinion, design really leads to the creation of great products or experiences through a deeper understanding of people and their needs. The design process is useful not only for building these great products and solutions, but rather, for pursuing a more creative and open-minded approach to life. Marketing creates interest in a brand, but design communicates a brand. All in all I believe my interpersonal, collaborative, and business skill set along with the tools I gained through the UX/UI course allows me to confidently present work to a company's stakeholders while championing and creating change in future products and their users.
