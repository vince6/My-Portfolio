Summary

With such a concentrated market among cloud storage applications it’s difficult for users to select one application that meets all their personal and professional needs.These existing platforms are coming up short when it comes to updated designs, specific collaboration features, and content sharing methodologies.

Problem

Many of the cloud storage applications today often specialize in certain features and lack in others. The result is frustrated users having to utilize multiple applications to achieve different tasks. RainDrops strives to connect it’s users to their everyday functions all in one drop.

Solution

Through extensive research, user testing, and a simplistic design approach, I developed Raindrops. A cloud storage application that allows users to save, share, and collaborate all from one central location.

Research & Discovery

User Research

I created a 20 question user survey to better understand the users needs in a cloud storage application but also their frustations when using these types of programs. The following is what I learned from their responses:

85.7%
Of respondents said uploading content such as links, images, and files from a computer or mobile device was most important to them.

79. 5%
Of respondents prefer to use a desktop platform over a mobile platform.

64%
Of respondents want to see an updated design within their application.

85%
Of respondents were frustrated with the collaborative – sharing efficiency within their current cloud storage application.

Based on these key surveys and personas I narrowed down the project objectives to create an application that conveniently allows users to upload content from the web to their application, create an effective yet efficient way to collaborate over the application, an updated interface and design should be implemented for convenience and simple use, and focusing on accessibility from any Desktop.

Competitive Analysis



Learning from your competitors....

Although these are among the top players in the market, there are a few weaknesses that have been brought to light and could definitely be improved on. GoogleDrive is great with a generous amout of storage and a great brand to support it. However from my research many users wish it’s interface was more user friendly. DropBox, another powerful brand, has an awesome easy to use interface along with a fast uploading speeds, however the pricing after your free 5 GB can get pricey very quickly. OneDrive also has a simplistic interface and pretty cost effecient. However if one does not know how to take advantage of Microsoft 360 or a Microsoft user it defeats the purpose.

Branding & Identity

I conducting some brainstorming activities around what name and color palette would represent a new cloud storage application trying to penetrate the market. In doing so I came away with the name RainDrops. Raindrops represents what a cloud represents to planet earth. A storage application containing important files, folders, and tools, such as a cloud containing the crucial elements for earth, water.

Wireframes

After all the research and branding was completed it was time to mock up the low fidelity prototypes and wireframes. When creating these wireframes I knew I wanted to create something very simplistic and easy to use. There were many instances where I tried adding countless features trying to recreating the wheel, and we all know that hardly ever works. I went back to my findings and kept the minimal viable product (MVP) in mind and went to work.

Interative and Visual Design

Hi-Fidelity Mockups

The high-fedlity mockups were ready to be created, deployed, and tested.

Icon Dashboard

The Raindrops dashboard gives the user options in how they would like their page layout. Here is a smiple intuitive dashboard that uses icon to represent what time of content the user is holding within the dashboard.

List Dashboard

The list dashboard is a for users who prefer the description of their files along with the last time they modified it, who they shared it with and the size of the content. This also allows the user to view more files on one screen.

Sign in page

The sign in page is a simple straight forward form that asks for First and Last name, email, and a password. Users may also sign in with their facebook account if they feel more comfortable with this alternative.

Usability & Preference Testing

As I reached out to users via zoom video and in person testing, I explained what the goal of this test was and for them to do their best in walking me through their thoughts, next moves, and how each option/element made them feel over-all.

80% preferred the option B refinement saying it was well laid out and added more depth to the application. Users appreciated the drag and drop water mark that was centered in the screen notifying them it was an option.

Conclusion

What I’d do differently..

ALOT. Gathering the pivotal pieces for the solution and keeping tabs on every important detail of the project was a challenge. I wish I could go back and give each and every detail more thought and attention. Creating consistent visuals was difficult as well as brainstorming the brand and tangibles for the style guide.

What I learned..

This was my first UX project that I captained along with my mentor. I learned so many things around the process and the methods used in creating a product. I also learned that testing is your key to success. Test early and often and you’ll learn so many things you thought you already knew. Although this was a challenging project for me, I feel I took away key components from the design process and my next project will be a huge improvement from RainDrops.
